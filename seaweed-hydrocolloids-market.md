<p><p>The seaweed hydrocolloids market includes three key types: Agar-Agar, Alginates, and Carrageenan. Alginates, extracted from brown seaweed, are valued for their thickening and stabilizing abilities, commonly used in food, textiles, and wound care. Carrageenan, sourced from red seaweed, is a popular food additive for its emulsifying and thickening properties, extensively used in dairy and meat products. Each type serves diverse industries, driven by their unique functional characteristics.</p></p>
